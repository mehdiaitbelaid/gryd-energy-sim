The fleet · a virtual power plant

Flexibility the grid can call on.

One home is a few kilowatts. Pool many funded homes and the same constrained batteries become a dispatchable block of power Gryd can sell into the evening peak. Homes vary in size, region and panel direction, generated from a fixed seed so the fleet is reproducible.

How this maps to Gryd

The funded asset moves the optimisation boundary. The home gets a fixed discount on the bill it would otherwise pay; Gryd owns the tariff exposure, battery wear and hardware, and keeps the export and flexibility value. Pooled across homes, that flexibility is something Gryd can sell.

Data & method

Octopus Agile half-hourly prices, inc VAT. Solar irradiance from Open-Meteo (hourly, applied across each half hour).

Defaults follow Gryd's real spec (~5.4 kWh battery, ~4 kWp); hardware capex scales with system size. The home annual sums every available day of 2025 (DST transition days excluded); fleet annual figures are scaled from representative days and labelled estimates.

Assumptions

Hardware £6,500 over Gryd's 25-year guarantee + 2.5%/yr upkeep (covers a mid-life battery swap). Round-trip efficiency 88%. Standing charge £0.60/day. Flex is an evening-window availability estimate capped at inverter power. Home photo is an AI-generated illustration.
